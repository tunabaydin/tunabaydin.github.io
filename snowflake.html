Snowflake

Chocolate wrapper paper · 2023 · 41.5 × 75.8 cm

The main inspiration for this piece was repurposing chocolate packaging. There are two components to the packaging: a hard colorful paper and a thinner shiny paper. Both contain patterns that I cut, outlined with black marker, and arranged into the final composition. The main inspiration for the art was repurposing the chocolate packaging. There are 2 components to the packaging, a hard colorful paper and a thinner shiny paper, both containing patterns. I also used a black marker and glue to put the pieces together.

Each section corresponds to a different source of chocolate, forming small “families” within the work. Together they create a mandala-like pattern, symbolizing different people and moments gathered around the same shared experiences – just like a unique snowflake. There are 5 different patterns which corresponds to 5 different flavors of chocolate. They all form an individual "family" within. Being opposite to each other, symbolizing 2 parents we inherit genetic (and sometimes cultural) material from. They all originate from the same source and form a unique mandala style pattern. Just like a unique snowflake.
